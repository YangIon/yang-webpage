PERSEVERANCE IN EVERY MOMENT

To the profiteer of my suffering

W ell I googled perseverance because it was the first word that came to mind when I thought of her. So let's talk about perseverance for a bit.

Google defines it as “steadfastness in doing something despite difficulty or delay in achieving success.” At times, it’s just a remarkably nice-sounding buzzword that others like to use to convince you to do something you may not realistically be able to do. Sometimes, that mentioning of perseverance is a point of weakness—my point of weakness—because it often implies that there will be major difficulty rather than can be major difficulty along the undefined amount of time it takes to achieve whatever made such a troublesome perseverance worth it in the first place. And in some guise of being technical about it, how are you to know if the benefits outweigh the costs?

So perseverance can be something to be feared or something to be loved; the effort and the passion directed towards a meaningful goal are the admirable parts—otherwise it would be insanity.

For her, perseverance might mean pulling several all-nighters to paint the most insightful portrait of every topic she covers as a journalist, editing several underclassmen college essays—and a friend's PhD application essay—at last minute requests, or being a constant presence for when a random, troubled writer came knocking on her door for late night advice, coffee excluded. Quaint.

She is her own art. The graceful dancer powering through weekly several-hour dance performances on a "more than sore" ankle, the empowering activist organizing one of the largest marches the city of Greensboro (otherwise "Greensboring") has ever witnessed, the humble soul whose actions are all done with an absurdly consistent modesty that everyone else around her comes first in spite of the responsibilities she assumes.

And it's fascinating to me, knowing that each person experiences some form of adversity within themselves, that she does all this with the easiest smile to her face, straight whites invisibly laced with straight confidence.

Often when I meet her, I get a little-maybe-more-than-a-little concerned for her. Knowing only a fraction of the whole works she puts herself through, I don't imagine I could pull it off. Sometimes life throws a storm at her and she's at the helm of a ship that has to break through the waves. A person like that can only be described as strong and inspiring, but it can be a little... taxing.

Because in those moments when we want to be our picture-best, we might find it easy to abstract our problems under a happy face or an off-handed remark rather than confront them right then and there. So how she embraces perseverance within multiple ends of her life: externally, internally, perhaps something in between those two(?) is something of a great lesson I've learned from her. That's the way I see it as her friend.

Full of calm smiles, excited faces, and mocking expressions at her own doubts, she's a really consoling sort of character. A calming presence who wears her strength casually, holding to bubbly, effervescent conversations which lend people a lot more assurance in whatever they do, eliciting a perseverance beyond herself. I'm reminded of the small things, the minute details she puts care into: the coffee past hours at 2 a.m., abridged recitations of her favorite Hindu fables, artsy handwritten letters to teachers, friends, and family.

Personally, I appreciate her for all the talks we've had in the couple years we've known each other. Each one has built my personal connection towards reflecting on meaningful relationships, emotional insecurity, self-love—just a few items that I find it incredibly simple to talk to her about because she is just there in the moment, sharing out of a mode for compassionate feelings over convenience. Knowing that she has instilled in my life a library of lessons that comfort me to fully live, laugh, and love, I find it difficult to ever write about a friendship that has meant a little more than the world to me.
